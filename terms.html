Introduction

The Ace Digital Product Passport (DPP) Vocabulary is a comprehensive semantic vocabulary designed to support the European Union's Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation (ESPR). This vocabulary provides standardized terms for describing product information, sustainability metrics, lifecycle data, and compliance documentation.

This vocabulary is designed to be:

  • ESPR-Compliant: Covers all requirements from ESPR Articles 4-12
  • Interoperable: Uses established vocabularies like Schema.org where applicable
  • Machine-Readable: Full JSON-LD and RDF support
  • Extensible: Can be extended for industry-specific requirements

Additional Terms

The vocabulary includes many additional specialized terms. Key categories include:

  • Verification and Quality: verificationBody, pcfDataQualityRating, dataQuality, accuracy, completeness
  • End of Life: recyclabilityRate, dismantlingInstructions, takeBackScheme, eprContact, wasteClassification
  • Metadata: resolverUrl, registryEntryId, accessRightsMetadata, interoperability, storageLocation
  • Custom Claims: carbonFootprintVerified, recycledContentVerified, certificationLevel (for Verifiable Credentials)
